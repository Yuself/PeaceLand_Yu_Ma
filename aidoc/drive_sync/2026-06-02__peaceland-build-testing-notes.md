# Pre-Summer 2026 - Build Notes

**Build 7/24/25**

- Dialogues feel monotonic at some points; need to make them more fitting for the situation, context, and the people (dialects)
- To show a more direct contrast between the memory and the present, we should alter the characters' voices. There is a difference in age, experiences, emotions, sufferings, and the dialogue tones and voices should match and capture that feeling.
- We should also try to alter the voices/terms to match some regional slang that match character's personalities.
- The thorn-cutting mini-game makes it more interactive and interesting. To fully capture the "game essence", we should add more such small mini-games. Overall, very nice change of pace.
- The snap into place for the silhouettes feels a little off here, but very minor critique.
- Mira's inner voices are very succinct and great to add more layers and depth to her character.
- ALSO, CANNOT STRESS THIS ENOUGH - A background score is required. That is a priority if you want to truly immerse yourself in the narrative. (I played some background music from a visual novel I read while going through this at some parts, and it made a tremendous amount of impact.)
- So far, amazing work getting through this. The blurred-out effect when she starts to tear up is a very good touch. The premise is also very strong and layered. I like how there is some information left out, and the reader is not bombarded with a lot of knowledge.
- To add to the above point, we can do a lot with the text itself. Adding effects and animations to it would improve interactivity.

Very strong premise and plot. Thoroughly enjoyed what I read, even though it was just the setup, and it left me wanting more. Great job here, guys.

**Build 4/24/26**

- Love the new art style and main menu. Amazing job on the title text on that screen.
- The mouse hover has changed, and the art style (again) looks very nice. I like the way the text box looks.
- Love the start of this, setting up a dynamic between two characters with some sense of mystique in the background.
- The letter-assembling mini-game is a cool touch, and I like the way it has been integrated into the narrative flow.
- The memory felt very short (good enough for a demo showcase), but in that short time, it sets up 3 characters, and the world around them. It also introduces the conflict and the overarching emotional turnpoint of the memory without direct exposition.
- A very tight and interesting narrative is set up there, with plenty of ground to explore.
- AGAIN MUSIC!!!
- The dialogues in this one felt way more direct. Still, the same considerations as the previous build, some linguistic diversity, flair, and style would hit home.
- The mini-game in this one was much more limited and wasn't around for long.

Overall, very pleasant results, very excited to have a say and contribute to this already wonderful project.

With a game like this, it would be great to introduce an undo/redo option, or a history log of the dialogue and flair text.
